How to Lower the Risk of Heart Disease

Heart disease is a serious condition, but there are many ways to lower your risk. Here are some tips:

  • Eat a heart-healthy diet: Focus on eating more fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid saturated and trans fats.
  • Exercise regularly: Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate physical activity most days of the week.
  • Maintain a healthy weight: Losing weight if you're overweight can reduce the strain on your heart and lower your risk of heart disease.
  • Avoid smoking: Smoking increases the risk of heart disease by damaging blood vessels and reducing oxygen in the blood.
  • Control blood pressure: High blood pressure can damage the heart and arteries. Regular check-ups and medications (if necessary) are essential.
  • Manage stress: Chronic stress can affect your heart health, so make sure to take time to relax and unwind.
